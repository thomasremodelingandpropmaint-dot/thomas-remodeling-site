Rental Turnover Repair Checklist for Philadelphia Landlords

A practical checklist for Philadelphia landlords preparing a unit for rent: paint, drywall, fixtures, leaks, safety, photos, and punch-list priorities.

Why turnovers matter

Every week a rental unit sits vacant costs money. A clean, organized turnover plan helps Philadelphia landlords rent faster, reduce tenant complaints, and avoid last-minute repair chaos.

Fast turnover checklist

  • Patch drywall holes and wall damage
  • Touch up or repaint high-traffic rooms
  • Replace broken blinds, curtain rods, or door hardware
  • Check faucets, toilets, and under-sink leaks
  • Replace damaged outlet covers and basic fixtures
  • Verify smoke detectors are present and working
  • Clean, stage, and photograph the unit before showing

Prioritize visible repairs first

Prospective tenants notice walls, floors, lighting, kitchen condition, bathroom condition, odors, and broken items immediately. Cosmetic repairs can improve rental appeal quickly when done before photos and showings.

Don’t ignore small leaks

A small toilet leak, loose supply line, or dripping faucet can become expensive if ignored. During turnover, it is smart to check plumbing basics while the unit is empty.

Use photos for faster quotes

For smaller landlord punch lists, texting photos of each issue can speed up pricing and scheduling. Grouping several small repairs into one visit usually costs less than handling one item at a time.
