How Much Does Faucet Replacement Cost in Philadelphia?

A homeowner guide to faucet replacement costs, what affects pricing, and when simple plumbing jobs become more complicated.

Typical faucet replacement factors

Faucet replacement pricing depends on the faucet type, access under the sink, condition of shutoff valves, supply lines, drain components, and whether the customer already purchased the faucet.

Common reasons pricing changes

  • Old or stuck shutoff valves
  • Rusted mounting hardware
  • Leaking supply lines
  • Tight access under the sink
  • Drain assembly replacement
  • Kitchen faucet vs bathroom faucet differences

Why photos help

Photos of the faucet, underside of the sink, shutoff valves, and new faucet box can help confirm whether the job looks simple or may need extra parts.

When to call a licensed plumber

Some minor faucet replacements are straightforward. But if there are active leaks inside walls, major pipe changes, or old shutoff valves that will not work, a licensed plumber may be needed.

Best way to prepare

Before the appointment, clear everything from under the sink, confirm the new faucet is on site, and take photos of any existing leaks or corrosion.
