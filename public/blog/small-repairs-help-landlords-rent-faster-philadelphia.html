Small Repairs That Help Philadelphia Landlords Rent Units Faster

Small, affordable repairs that can improve rental appeal for Philadelphia landlords before listing a unit.

Small repairs can change first impressions

Landlords do not always need a full renovation to improve rental appeal. Often, a clean punch list of small repairs helps the unit photograph better, show better, and feel more move-in ready.

High-impact small repairs

  • Patch visible wall damage
  • Touch up paint in high-traffic areas
  • Replace broken blinds or curtain rods
  • Upgrade damaged outlet covers
  • Fix loose door handles and cabinet hardware
  • Replace basic light fixtures
  • Stop toilet or faucet leaks
  • Clean and stage the main rooms

Focus on what tenants notice

Tenants notice lighting, walls, floors, odors, leaks, bathroom cleanliness, kitchen condition, and whether doors and fixtures work properly. Those items should be addressed before photos or showings.

Bundle repairs into one visit

Bundling multiple small tasks into one visit is usually more efficient than scheduling separate trips. It also makes it easier for the landlord to track what was completed.

Use a punch-list system

Walk the unit room by room, take photos, and create a list. A simple photo-based punch list makes it easier to estimate, schedule, and complete the work quickly.
